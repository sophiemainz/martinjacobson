Martin Jacobson (*14 June 1994 in Uppsala) is a freshly baked doctorate, political theorist, philosopher and left-libertarian Geoist. His work is considered the foundation of Jacobsonain left-liberal Geoism, a theoretical school that has been highly popularized after the publication of Jacobson’s first book Land & Liberty: On the Natural Monopoly of Violence (2024). Due to the similarities to the first popular movement of Geoism after Henry George’s book Progress and Poverty (1879), Jacobsonian left-libertarian Geoism is often also referred to as second-wave Geoism.

Martin Jacobson is also a private tour guide of the city of Uppsala.

Biography

Jacobson was born in Uppsala in Uppsala County, Sweden on 14 June 1994. His family has long roots in the city of Uppsala, with one of his relatives having lived at Gamla Torget, in the building that is nowadays home to the Department of Government of Uppsala University.

Jacobson attended Uppsala’s Katedralskolan, before studying Political Science and Philosophy at Uppsala University. During his time as a student, he worked in Uppsala’s museums, fostering his deep-rooted interest in his hometown, history and power struggles.

During his student years, Jacobson formulated three impactful theses:

  • Bachelor thesis: Self-ownership and the Foundations of Libertarianism: Applying Kymlicka’s Arguments on Geolibertarianism
  • Magister thesis: Power, Paternalism and Libertarianism: Libertarian Paternalism – More than a Nudge
  • Master thesis: Anarchy, State and the Political Conception of Justice

From August 2019 on, he began working on his dissertation as a doctoral candidate of Jonas Hultin Rosenberg and Johan Tralau. Unfortunately, his time as a doctoral student fell into the global Covid-19 pandemic which was particularly tragic for Jacobson who is known for his commitment to his peers and his social nature.

Jacobson defended his dissertation on 7 June 2024, with a deep and elaborate discussion led by the famous contemporary political theorist Robert Huseby, professor at the University of Oslo.

The following traditional defense party at Västgöta nation became known as one of the city's most intense and glorious festivities since the famous parties by Michel Foucault during his time in Uppsala in the 1950s.

Personality

Martin Jacobson became known for his ability to show up for others and his loyalty to his friends and peers. Being an organizer of the regular “No Doodle - No Bullshit” (NDNB) after-work, he plays a crucial role in unifying the PhD community and in the peer-therapy sessions doctoral candidates commonly engage in.

Jacobson has further been described by his cohort friend Luise Bendfeldt as “always supportive and an integral part of his doctoral cohort bringing interesting perspectives and encouragement about everyone’s ideas”.

He is further esteemed and appreciated for living his political ideas and values even in his private life, as he has been committed to not harming animals and a vegan lifestyle for several years.
